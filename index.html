Belgium · 16th century

Flemish Giant

Gentle Docile Loyal
weight6 – 10 kg
lifespan5 – 8 years
coatShort, dense
careModerate

The undisputed king of rabbit breeds. Flemish Giants are remarkably mellow despite their size — patient with children, tolerant of handling, and surprisingly social. They need room to roam and thrive on companionship.

did you know

Their nickname is "the dog of the rabbit world" — they follow their owners around like a loyal pup.

Belgium · 1990s

Lionhead

Playful Bold Curious
weight1.3 – 1.7 kg
lifespan7 – 10 years
coatLong mane + short body
careDaily grooming

Small in stature, enormous in personality. The Lionhead's dramatic mane is the result of a single genetic mutation — and the attitude that comes with it is equally outsized. They love to explore, demand attention, and occasionally judge you.

did you know

A kitten needs two copies of the mane gene for a full mane. One copy only creates a "single mane" that thins with age.

Netherlands · 1950s

Holland Lop

Affectionate Sociable Playful
weightup to 2 kg
lifespan7 – 12 years
coatShort, dense
careEasy

Consistently ranked the most popular pet rabbit in the world. Their oversized floppy ears and round puppy face are almost unfairly cute — matched by a warm, genuinely people-loving temperament that makes them easy to adore.

did you know

Bred by Dutch enthusiast Adriann de Cock in the 1950s by crossing French Lops with Netherland Dwarfs — an inspired combination.

04

Ankara, Turkey · ancient breed

Angora

Calm Gentle Patient
weight2 – 3.5 kg
lifespan7 – 12 years
coatLong wool, requires daily care
careHigh — daily grooming

A living cloud. The English Angora produces the finest, softest wool of any animal — up to 1 kg per year. Their zen-like temperament makes daily grooming sessions feel meditative. Just don't ever skip them.

did you know

Angora wool is seven times warmer than sheep wool, and was once reserved exclusively for royalty and the very wealthy.

05

France · 1880s

Harlequin

Playful Inquisitive Easygoing
weight2.5 – 3.5 kg
lifespan5 – 8 years
coatShort, low-maintenance
careEasy

A work of living art. Show judges specifically look for a perfect 50/50 bilateral color split — but Harlequins are too busy exploring and making you smile to care about symmetry.

did you know

No two Harlequins have identical markings. Each rabbit's pattern is as unique as a fingerprint.

06

Netherlands · early 20th century

Netherland Dwarf

Spirited Alert Independent
weight0.9 – 1.1 kg
lifespan10 – 12 years
coatShort, dense
careModerate

The smallest recognized rabbit breed in the world — and one of the most opinionated. They carry a wild rabbit gene that makes them boldly disproportionate in personality to their size. Approach with respect.

did you know

Their compact jaw can cause dental issues. Despite their otherwise hardy constitution, regular vet checkups are essential.
